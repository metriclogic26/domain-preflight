Glossary

DNS & email security terms

Short definitions for SPF, DKIM, DMARC, and related concepts. Each entry links to the DomainPreflight tool that helps you verify or fix the issue.

  • SPF Record

    DNS TXT that lists which mail servers may send email for your domain; subject to a 10-lookup limit.

  • DKIM

    Digital signatures on outgoing mail, verified with a public key in DNS.

  • DMARC

    Policy for SPF/DKIM failures plus aggregate reporting (rua).

  • DMARC Alignment

    Requirement that From: matches SPF or DKIM domain — often breaks with third-party senders.

  • PTR Record

    Reverse DNS (IP → hostname); critical for inbox delivery.

  • Email Spoofing

    Forging the From: header; mitigated by SPF, DKIM, and DMARC.

  • Subdomain Takeover

    Dangling CNAMEs to deprovisioned cloud apps that attackers can reclaim.

  • Typosquatting

    Lookalike domains for phishing and brand abuse.

Monitoring Domain Expiry — Dynadot

Know where renewal settings live for Dynadot, then cross-check the public expiry with WHOIS.

Where to look

My Domains — most registrars show expiry on the domain dashboard. Names differ: Renew, Expires, Registration.

Step by step

Step 1 Sign in to Dynadot.
Step 2 Open your domain’s registration or overview page (path varies by brand).
Step 3 Note the expiry / renewal date shown in the panel.
Step 4 Turn on auto-renew if you want uninterrupted registration.
Step 5 Ensure a valid payment method is on file.
Step 6 Run DomainPreflight WHOIS to see registry-reported expiry and risk tier.

FAQ

Why doesn’t the panel match WHOIS exactly?

Registry timing vs cache — WHOIS is authoritative for public expiry; the panel shows what the registrar plans.

What if auto-renew fails?

Update billing early — some TLDs have short redemption windows.

Can I transfer away from Dynadot before expiry?

Usually yes — unlock / auth code steps depend on the registrar.

Does DNS stop if the domain expires?

Eventually yes — the name leaves DNS when the registration lapses beyond grace.

What is DomainPreflight risk tier?

A quick signal from WHOIS data — still verify in your registrar account.
